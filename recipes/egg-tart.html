Hong Kong Style Egg Tart

Description

The egg tart, or dan tat in Cantonese, is a beloved Chinese pastry with influences from the English custard tart and Portuguese pastel de nata. It is commonly served at dim sum restaurants and bakeries. This recipe for a Hong Kong style egg tart is a perfect combination of light, creamy custard and buttery, flaky crust.

Ingredients

Custard Filling

  • 4 eggs, beaten--reserve 2 tablespoons for your pastry dough
  • 3/4 cup water, hot
  • 6 tablespoons sugar
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup evaporated milk

Pastry Dough

  • 2 cups cake flour
  • 115 grams unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons egg, beaten
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, sift flour, sugar, and salt. Add softened butter. Bring the mixture together with your hands.
  2. Whisk the egg yolks, and add the 2 tablespoons of beaten yolk to the flour mixture. Bring together until smooth. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  3. Melt sugar and salt with hot water. Mix until dissolved and let cool.
  4. Add the rest of the beaten egg yolk. Stir in sugar water and evaporated milk. Combine well.
  5. Strain the filling to ensure there are no lumps. Chill in the refrigerator.
  6. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit.
  7. Take the dough out and divide into 16 equal portions. Spray the tart pan with a light coating of oil. Take one portion of your dough and roll it into a ball and place in your tart shell. Press the shell into the pan with your fingers. Repeat to finish all.
  8. Pour the custard filling into the shells until about 80% full. Bake for 15-20 minutes until the surface becomes golden brown.
  9. Cool down for several minutes, and then remove the egg tarts from the pan.
